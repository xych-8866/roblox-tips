Part 1: The Market Fundamentals

Supply & Demand Dynamics

Every pet in Adopt Me has value based on two factors: supply (how many exist) and demand (how many players want it). Understanding this is the foundation of all trading.

  • High Supply, Low Demand: Value drops (e.g., common pets).
  • Low Supply, High Demand: Value rises (e.g., Shadow Dragon, Bat Dragon).
  • Medium Supply, Medium Demand: Value stable (e.g., Frost Dragon, Owl).

Part 2: Advanced Flipping Strategies

Strategy 1: The 4-for-1 Upgrade

Trade 4 low-tier legendaries for 1 mid-tier legendary. Then trade that mid-tier + adds for a high-tier. Repeat until you reach God-Tier.

Example: 4 Unicorns → 1 Turtle → Turtle + Ride Potion → 1 Frost Dragon → Frost + Adds → Shadow Dragon.

Strategy 2: Event Arbitrage

Buy event pets during the event when prices are low. Hold for 3-6 months. Sell when prices spike before the next event.

Example: Golden Mermaid bought for 50B during Summer 2026, sold for 150B+ after 3 months.

Strategy 3: Potion Flipping

Ride and Fly potions are consistent currency. Buy potions for 1-2 legendary pets, trade them for 3-4 legendary pets during peak demand (weekends, events).

Strategy 4: Neon Crafting

Buy 4 full-grown pets (or age them yourself), craft a neon, sell for 2-3x the cost. Mega neons sell for 4-5x.

Part 4: Trading Psychology

The 3 Golden Rules

  • Rule 1: Never rush – the best deals take time.
  • Rule 2: Don't get attached – pets are assets, not friends.
  • Rule 3: Check value lists before every trade – always.

Common Mistakes

  • Overpaying for a pet you want – always ask for adds.
  • Accepting the first offer – the first is rarely the best.
  • Ignoring demand – value isn't everything, demand drives prices.
  • Falling for scams – never trust trades, always use the trade window.

Pro Tips

  • Use multiple Discord servers – different servers have different value lists – find the best deals.
  • Trade during events – players sell cheap to get event currency.
  • Check the auction house – sometimes pets sell for less than market price.
  • Be patient – the best trades take weeks, not minutes.
